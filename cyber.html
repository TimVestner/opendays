Cognitive Tricks

  • ▸▾Anchoring Effect

    We evaluate prices by the surrounding context. A price will seem lower in the context of high prices, or higher in the context of low prices. This is used to make prices appear more attractive than they are.

  • ▸▾Default Effect

    People have a bias to accept default options - this decreases the amount of decisions they need to make. This is used to get people to buy more when they would have chosen less.

  • ▸▾Goal Gradient

    People are more likely to stick with something until they have finished if they can see their progress towards the goal.

  • ▸▾Social Proof

    When people make a decision, they tend to look at what decisions others have made and use that to influence their own choice. This is used to steer people towards popular choices.
