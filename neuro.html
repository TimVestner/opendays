Disorders

Click for details, then click the corresponding image

  • ▸▾Alzheimer's Disease

    Alzheimer's affects the entire brain, especially memory and thinking. Certain proteins don't get removed and cause the brain to shrink.

  • ▸▾Brain Tumors

    Brain Tumors are abnormal growths in the brain. Not all of them are dangerous and how they affect a person depends on their location.

  • ▸▾Parkinson's Disease

    Parkinson's Disease affects areas deep within the brain that are responsible for movement. The cells in these areas have trouble talking to each other because they have less of the chemicals that they use for communication.

  • ▸▾Stroke - Hemorrhagic

    In hemorrhagic strokes, blood vessels start bleeding, which means that the areas connected to these vessels don't get enough blood and can't function properly.

  • ▸▾Stroke - Ischemic

    In ischemic strokes, blood vessels get blocked, which means that the areas connected to these vessels don't get enough blood and can't function properly.
